# 🦸‍♀️ Superheroine Fatigue

## Background

*Superhero fatigue* is a growing exhaustion among the general public around the superhero movies. This is a stark change change from the era of Marvel's Avengers where everyone was essentially trampling over each other to be seated for the first screening of The Avengers: End Game. 

A proposed reason for this fatigue is to do with the sheer amount of superhero content across various mediums. For example, in Phase 2 (Hydra & The Infinity Stones; 2012-2015) the Marvel Cinematic Universe, audiences are expected to have watched Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D. (TV Show; Season 1), Iron Man 3 (Movie), Thor: The Dark World (Movie), All Hail the King (Special), Captain America: Winter Soldier (Movie), Guardians of the Galaxy (Movie), Guardians of the Galaxy (Movie) and Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D. (TV Show; Season 2) in this specific order and this was the shortest phase chosen! The longest was Phase 4 (Multiverse Opens) with 27 seperate pieces of content spread between 2024 and 2025 - only two years! ([source](https://marvelwatchlist.com/watch-order/)). 

Therefore, it is no wonder why superhero fatigue has been regularly documented by various outlets, including [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/markhughes/2024/01/04/what-really-caused-superhero-fatigue-in-2023-and-how-to-fix-it/) and [Collider](https://collider.com/superhero-fatigue-is-real-ways-to-fight-it/). Although it has been refuted by some, with Peter Safran, the co-CEO of DC Studios alongside James Gunn reframing superhero fatigue as "mediocre movie fatigue" and that audiences are craving for something "cool and original" like the latest Supergirl (2026) movie ([IGN](https://www.ign.com/articles/dc-studios-co-ceo-says-superhero-fatigue-was-never-a-thing-just-mediocre-movie-fatigue-points-to-supergirl-as-something-cool-and-original)).

However, his idea that Supergirl, the second movie of the Detective Comics Extended Universe (DCEU), would wake everyone up from their superhero fatigue was not proven true, given the movie did not meet Box Office openings expectations as [The Hollywood Reporter](https://www.hollywoodreporter.com/movies/movie-news/supergirl-opening-box-office-dc-studios-peter-safran-1236632757/) reports. 

Although, it is difficult to see how this unsuccess can be attributed to the initially proposed reason for superhero fatigue; overwhelming releases. This is the second DCEU release, with the first, Superman (2025), premiering a little less than a year ago. Looking more generally, Hollywood released 10 pieces of superhero-specific content (including movies and TV shows) in 2025 and are looking to release four in 2026 with only Supergirl already released (at the time of writing: 2nd July 2026). This is not an exhaustive number of content.

Thus, could there be an alternative reason why this movie did not deliver as expected?
