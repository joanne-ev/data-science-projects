# 🦸‍♀️ Superheroine Fatigue

## Background

*Superhero fatigue* is a growing exhaustion among the general public around the superhero movies. This is a stark change change from the era of Marvel's Avengers where everyone was essentially trampling over each other to be seated for the first screening of End Game. 

A proposed reason for this fatigue is to do with the sheer amount of superhero content across various mediums. For example, in Phase 2 (Hydra & The Infinity Stones; 2012-2015) the Marvel Cinematic Universe, audiences are expected to have watched Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D. (TV Show; Season 1), Iron Man 3 (Movie), Thor: The Dark World (Movie), All Hail the King (Special), Captain America: Winter Soldier (Movie), Guardians of the Galaxy (Movie), Guardians of the Galaxy (Movie) and Agents of S.H.I.E.L.D. (TV Show; Season 2) in this specific order and this was the shortest phase chosen! The longest is Phase 4 (Multiverse Opens) with 27 seperate pieces of content spread between 2024 and 2025 - only two years! ([source](https://marvelwatchlist.com/watch-order/)). 

Thus, there is no wonder why superhero fatigue has been regularly documented by various outlets, including [Forbes](https://www.forbes.com/sites/markhughes/2024/01/04/what-really-caused-superhero-fatigue-in-2023-and-how-to-fix-it/) and [Collider](https://collider.com/superhero-fatigue-is-real-ways-to-fight-it/). Although it has been refuted by some, with Peter Safran, the co-CEO of DC Studios alongside James Gunn reframing superhero fatigue as "mediocre movie fatigue" and that audiences are looking for something different, suggesting the latest Supergirl (2026) movie was the something "cool and original" that audiences were craving ([IGN](https://www.ign.com/articles/dc-studios-co-ceo-says-superhero-fatigue-was-never-a-thing-just-mediocre-movie-fatigue-points-to-supergirl-as-something-cool-and-original)). 

However, his idea that Supergirl, the second movie of the Detective Comics Extended Universe (DCEU) would wake everyone up from their superhero fatigue was not proven true, as Supergirl did not rise above in the Box Office openings. Although, could there be an alternate reason why this movie did not deliver as expected?
